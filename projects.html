KTH Racing

Together with an amazing team of people, I played a small role in designing and constructing the R10E, an FSAE formula racing car. I developed an innovative aerodynamics package with high focus on ground effects and high-downforce features, developing massive downforce at relatively low speeds.

Here is a short excerpt from an article about the KTH Racing project (now KTH Formula Student), showing the rendered car.

Details

Aerodynamic design

The package utilized an aggressive diffuser, large underbody air intake, a lower single-element rear wing and a large dual-element upper rear wing, as well as a small front wing.

The ground effects accounted for more than 50% of the total downforce, with the rear wings not only providing independent downforce, but also playing a crucial role in total underbody effects.

At top speeds, downforce should have exceeded weight.


Construction

The vehicle was fully constructed at competition, but sadly the powertrain was not fully completed in time.
